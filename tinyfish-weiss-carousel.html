Identity test

Jim and James were kept separate.

The demo did not jump to the romantic answer. It built two public profiles first, then compared overlap, contradictions, and missing bridge evidence.

Strongly supported, not publicly confirmed.

A = Jim McCabe

  • Named by Weiss in the Finnegans Wake thesis trail.
  • Linked to University College Dublin lectures on the Wake.
  • Appears in the Books Ireland / Thomas Kinsella orbit.

B = Dr James McCabe

  • Dublin-born, UCD educated, later Oxford doctorate.
  • Publicly associated with classical story practice.
  • Story Parlour and Kinsella/Mangan-adjacent traces match the same world.
Verdict: high-confidence hypothesis, with the public bridge still missing.
Stress test

The opposing case was allowed in.

Same-name traps are real. The evidence ladder explicitly preserves H0 and H2: different people, or different people in the same Dublin/UCD/Joyce/Kinsella circle.

This is why the demo stays credible.

For H1

  • Right field.
  • Right city.
  • Right institutions.
  • Right literary orbit.

Against H1

  • Source says Jim, not James.
  • Other McCabes exist.
  • No public aka bridge found.

Clean claim

  • Public evidence supports.
  • Private confirmation can close.
  • Keep layers separate.
